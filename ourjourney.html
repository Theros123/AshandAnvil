The struggle to find clothes that fit well

Eric Huang

December 31, 2014

Short men face an uphill battle when attempting to find clothing that fits their unique height and size. There are such things as big and tall stores for men, but nothing focusing on guys 5 foot 8 inches and shorter. Shopping for clothes is like gambling, every brand has a different length and cut. The best that shorter guys can hope for is to find a specific item for a brand that fits them well. For example, Steven has found that Docker’s casual button-downs have just the right length that fit and can be worn untucked.

An underserved clothing niche

With 25% of all men in the U.S., 5 feet 8 inches and below, one might wonder where are the brands catering to this niche? We’ve already talked about big and tall stores. Women have petite brands as well as plus sized ones.

Buying all clothing, including pants, shirts, jeans, etc, is difficult in today’s clothing brands. Off-the-rack items may fit size wise, but will probably be way too long. The consequence of the typical “small, medium, large, x-large” sizing that scales up width and length together. No guy wants to look ridiculous with arm-sail sleeves and baggy pants.

The lack of good solutions

At Ash & Anvil, we are acutely aware of the fact that there just aren’t many good solutions for shorter guys. Steven has struggled to find clothing that fits his entire adult life, having to resort to getting almost everything altered by a tailor. Here’s a few ways that we’ve found that might work for shorter guys to find clothing that fits:

1. Buy custom-made or tailored clothing only

An expensive option, custom clothes should fit the best. However, most guys can’t and shouldn’t spend typically above $100 per item. Even altering off-the-rack clothes can add up at $15+ per item. You also have to find a tailor who you can trust, we’ve all had that really bad dry cleaning experience where your dress shirt comes out ruined.

2. Try your luck in a store or online

You might have better luck finding what will fit on the Internet. However, as we touched on before, every brand has a different style and fit. Shorter guys have to try on everything to make it fits. There are a few niche brands attempting to fulfil this niche.
